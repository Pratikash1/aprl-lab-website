About APRL

The Advanced Propulsion Research Laboratory focuses on the science and engineering of turbulent reacting flows in aerospace and energy systems. The lab vision is to enable clean, efficient, and resilient combustion and propulsion technologies by addressing questions at the intersection of fluid dynamics, chemistry, acoustics, and measurement science.

Current work spans low-emission gas turbine combustors, thermo-acoustic instability, flow–flame interaction, cavity-stabilized supersonic combustion, dual-mode ramjet/scramjet physics, and sustainable combustion for power generation.

Principal Investigator

Prof. Pratikash P. Panda
Assistant Professor, Department of Aerospace Engineering, IISc Bangalore

Prof. Panda works on thermo-acoustic instability in gas turbine combustors and rocket engines, hypersonics and high-speed combustion, fire dynamics and suppression, and optical diagnostics including gas-phase spectroscopy. He received his PhD and MS from Purdue University and has held appointments at Sandia National Laboratories, FM Global Research Center, and IISc.

Facilities and Capabilities

  • Optical diagnostics with high-speed imaging, intensified cameras, pulsed lasers, and pressure instrumentation
  • Time-resolved PIV and related flow diagnostics
  • Combustion diagnostics including chemiluminescence and spectroscopy
  • Gas turbine combustor rigs and full-scale sector combustor research capability
  • Direct-connect high-speed propulsion facility for supersonic combustion studies
  • Experimental infrastructure for sustainable combustion and emissions measurements
