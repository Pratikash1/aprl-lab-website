Gas Turbine Combustion and Thermo-Acoustics

APRL investigates thermo-acoustic instability, externally forced response, self-excited oscillations, flame topology transitions, intermittency, and blow-off in swirl-stabilized combustors. The lab has developed a unique three-swirl-injector unfolded annular sector configuration to study standing transverse acoustic forcing and its effect on swirling jets and flames.

The work has revealed bistability between columnar-jet and wall-jet states, nonlinear flame response under large-amplitude forcing, and the influence of injector flare angle on flame stability and vortex breakdown mechanisms.

Full-Scale Sector Combustor Flow Dynamics

The group has designed and commissioned an optically accessible sector of a full-scale annular combustor with realistic dome, liner, casing, and injector geometry. This facility enables time-resolved measurements in both primary and exit zones under elevated pressure conditions.

Research focuses on pattern factor, pressure drop, combustion efficiency, coherent structures, dilution-jet interaction, and exit-flow non-uniformity in practical RQL combustors.

High-Speed Propulsion and Hypersonics

APRL performs high-fidelity experiments on cavity-stabilized supersonic combustion, auto-ignition, flame stabilization, shock-train dynamics, and scram-to-ram mode transition in dual-mode ramjet/scramjet systems.

Time-synchronized Schlieren imaging, CH* chemiluminescence, and dynamic pressure measurements are used to study ignition reliability and the complex coupling between heat release, blockage, shocks, and fuel-jet dynamics.

Sustainable Combustion and Diagnostics

The lab also studies biomass combustion, oxy-fuel combustion, alkali emissions, and measurement strategies relevant to sustainable power generation. These efforts build on the lab’s broader strength in spectroscopy, laser diagnostics, and validation-quality measurements in harsh reacting environments.
